## Harry Dry Copywriting Framework

### Core Principle
**Problem → Agitate → Solve**
Not features. Not benefits. **Transformation.**

Harry Dry doesn't sell what you do — he sells what changes for the customer when you do it.

### The Framework Components

#### 1. **Problem (Identify the Pain)**
- What keeps your customer up at night?
- Not "you need a website" — "your competitors already have one"
- Not "we do CRO" — "your site visitors are leaving without buying"

**For Fidelis:**
- Business owner has a website, but it's not making money
- They're getting traffic but no conversions
- They're spending on ads with no system to capture leads
- They have products/services but no way to sell them online
- They feel stuck between DIY and hiring expensive agencies

#### 2. **Agitate (Make it Real)**
- Paint the cost of NOT solving it
- "Your competitor is winning customers you should be getting"
- "Every month you delay costs you X in lost revenue"
- Emotional, specific, visceral

**For Fidelis:**
- "While you're figuring it out, your competitors are closing deals"
- "50% of customers won't buy from you without a modern site"
- "You're probably burning money on ads that don't convert"
- "You built something great but you can't prove it online"

#### 3. **Solve (Show the Path)**
- Not features. **Outcomes.**
- Don't say "we build Shopify stores" — say "your products go from invisible to bestsellers"
- Don't say "we do CRO" — say "visitors stop leaving without buying"

**For Fidelis:**
- "We build sites that make money, not just look pretty"
- "Our clients see 250-360% ROI increases in their first month"
- "From zero to $4,000/month in revenue — that's the Fidelis difference"

## Copy Formulas (Harry Dry Approved)

### Hero Section
**Template:**
```
[PAIN POINT THEY HAVE]

[WHY IT MATTERS / COST OF NOT FIXING IT]

[OUTCOME YOU DELIVER]

[PROOF]
```

**Current (weak):**
"Maximize Conversions. Minimize Spend."

**Harry Dry version:**
```
Your Site Isn't Selling

While you sleep, competitors are closing the deals you should be getting.
50% of customers won't buy without a modern website.
And if your site isn't converting, you're burning money on ads.

We fix that.

Fidelis builds conversion-first websites. No fluff. No bullshit. Just results.

→ Our clients see 360% average ROI increases
→ Went from €0 to €4,000/month in month one
→ 50+ businesses. 200+ projects. 5/5 stars.
```

### Service Pages (not features)

**Bad:** "We build Shopify stores"
**Good:** "We turn your products into bestsellers"

**Bad:** "CRO & optimization"
**Good:** "Every visitor who leaves without buying is money lost. We fix that."

**Bad:** "Web design"
**Good:** "A site that looks good AND makes money"

## Copy Audit: Fidelis Current State

### ✅ What Works
- "Maximize Conversions. Minimize Spend" — direct value
- Social proof is strong (€0 to €4,000/month testimonial is gold)
- Case studies show outcomes (250% conversion increase)
- 5/5 from 50+ reviews — huge

### ⚠️ What Needs Agitation
- No urgency. No "why now?"
- No pain statement. No "your competitors are winning"
- Too many features (Shopify, custom websites, automation) — dilutes message
- "We explore your challenges" = boring. "We turn your site into a revenue machine" = action

### 🔴 What's Missing
- Specific problem. Who are you FOR?
- Cost of inaction. What happens if they DON'T hire you?
- Faster transformation story (4-6 weeks is fast — OWN that)

## How to Apply This Going Forward

### Content Checklist
- [ ] Every headline: Does it solve a problem or agitate pain?
- [ ] Every section: Why should they care? (Not what you do)
- [ ] Every CTA: What changes if they do this?
- [ ] Social proof: Quantified outcomes (% increase, $X in revenue, time saved)
- [ ] No jargon: "conversion optimization" = "stop losing customers at checkout"

### Writing Prompts for Curt

When you're writing copy for a page:
1. **What problem does this solve?** (e.g., "client has no sales system")
2. **What happens if they don't fix it?** (e.g., "$10k/month in lost revenue")
3. **What changes after they hire you?** (e.g., "website makes money while they sleep")
4. **What's the proof?** (e.g., "one client went from €0 to €4k/month")

Write the agitated version first. Then simplify. That's Harry Dry.
